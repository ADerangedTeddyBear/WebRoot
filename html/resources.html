Step-By-Step Guide To Implementing A Fingerprint Scanner In Your Android Application JetRuby Agency This article written by The JetRuby Agency on Jetruby.com is a step by step guide on how to implement a fingerprint scanner into an android application.

It focuses on the FingerprintAPI and classes within this API like FingerprintManager and FingerprintManagerCompat that are used for interfacing an android phone's fingerprint sensor with an application. For security, the use of Android KeyStore is shown to help keep fingerprint information secure. A KeyPairGenerator is created from KeyStore to implement an encryptor and decryptor that helps store fingerprint data. The article shows the code that is used to complete these tasks and explains in great detail the process of implementing this fingerprint sensor solution.

This article will be very useful as it explains both the concepts and code that I might need to know to implement fingerprint sensors in an application. Especially since I am considering using fingerprint sensors for upcoming mobile development projects. 		10

What is a Smartphone Alyssa Provazza This article written by Alyssa Provazza for Search Mobile Computing defines what smartphones are as well as the history behind the development of smartphones by different companies

The article starts off with a description of common uses for smartphones such as social media, payment methods, and personal health monitoring. It also talks about integration with other mobile devices like smartwatches to keep track of personal vitals. This is followed by a section on industrial uses and how their compact size gives them an advantage over desktops when it comes to having a device for emails.

The article also talks about built-in hardware such as screens and cameras. These go into great detail on how they work and the types of technologies that exist such as LCDs and OLED displays on mobile phones.

This article also talks about the different vendors of smartphones and trends that these vendors have ushered. Knowing these trends would give me a good idea on what kind of features to expect on newer phones so that I can design my apps to take advantage of them. A brief section on early smartphone history such as how smartphones emerged from earlier devices like PDAs during the late 20th and early 21st century and how the fusion of PDAs and cellphones done by big tech companies led to the eventual birth of smartphones that we see today.

Last but not least, the article compares Androids to iPhones. The article compares the software of the two operating systems as well as how development on both platforms differs. This will prove useful when developing for both Android and IOS devices. 		9
